Senior Scouting Advancement

Membership

Sing the Philippine National Anthem - Lupang Hinirang and recite the Panunumpa ng Katapatan sa Watawat and the Panatang Makabayan.

Recite the Scout Oath and Law, Scout Motto, the Senior Scout Slogan, and the Senior Scout Code.

Demonstrate how to execute the Scout Sign, the Scout Salute, and the Scout Handshake.

Describe the World Scout Emblem, the BSP Scout Badge and the Senior Scout Emblem.

Explorer

Senior Scout Ideals
Recite and explain why Scouts must live up to the Scout Oath and Law, the Senior Scout Code, the Scout Motto, and the Senior Scout Slogan.

Show how to properly execute a Scout Sign, Scout Salute and a Scout Handshake and explain when and where you to use it.

Discuss with your fellow Scouts, the significant meaning of the World Scout Emblem, the BSP Scout Badge and the Senior Scout Emblem.

Attend regular religious services with the members of your family.

Explain the symbolism of the Evolution of the Philippine Flag and demonstrate how to hoist, lower, display, fold, and show respect for the Philippine Flag.

Social Activities
Get to know your Crew members and fellow Scouts in the Outfit, discuss with them the traditions and practices that you have observed and explain to them your commitment as Scout in the Outfit.

Identify and explain the different functions and positions of Scouts and Adult Leaders in the Crew and Outfit respectively.

Demonstrate an appropraite table manner and etiquette to your fellow Scout and the Outfit Advisor.

Explain to your fellow Scouts and Leaders the duties and responsibilities in taking care of the Crew and Outfit tools, properties and equipment.

Undertake activities with your Crew and Outfit that will help promote an active and healthy lifestyle.

Demonstrate proper conduct in boarding and alighting from a public conveyance with a lady and/or an elderly person, and the proper decorum when you meet someone you know on the street.

Explain and demonstrate appropriate manners for online communication and include respectful communication practices when interacting with peers, elders, and others in virtual settings, as well as the respect and caution required when engaging with sensitive information.

Vocational Activities
Do some basic home repairs and improvement (e.g. home furniture, simple electricity wiring, cleaning of appliances, faucet, water tube system, etc.) to the satisfaction of your parents / guardians.

Save at least a minimum of one peso a day and use it to open a savings account in your preferred bank or e-wallet provider.

Service Activities
Togther with your Crew members, plan, organize and conduct 'Scout's Good Turn' habit through a School or Community Service Project.

Outdoor and Indoor Activities
Plan, organize and conduct a 2-5 kilometers exploration activity (e.g. Rural, Urban, Historical, Ecological, Day, etc.). Explore and share your learning experiences while doing this activity.

Earn the following merit badges:
• Safety
• Citizenship
• Physical Fitness
• Ropework
• Cooking

Pathfinder

Senior Scout Ideals
Narrate your life experience wherein you were able to show examples of living up to the Scout Oath and Law, the Senior Scout Code, the Scout Motto, Senior Scout Slogan, and Mission and Vision.

Show proof of your spiritual growth and demonstrate faithfulness to your religious obligations.

Together with your Crew members in your school, volunteer to lead in at least three (3) flag-raising and flag-lowering ceremonies during Philippine Flag Day and/or Scouting Month.

Social Activities
Identify and explain with your Crew members the different functions of the Outfit Program Committees and how they help the Crew in accomplishing their advancement requirements.

Discuss with your friends, peers, and fellow Scouts the harmful effects of illegal drugs and substance abuse, including alcoholism and smoking. Explain why young people engage in these activities and how you can help avoid and prevent them.

Discuss with your peers, friends, and fellow Scouts how teenagers must behave in social parties at home, school, and communities, especially when interacting with members of the opposite sex.

Explain how to show courtesy and respect for the rights and properties of others.

Show proof that you are actively participating in sports activities with your Crew and Outfit that promote a healthy lifestyle.

Vocational Activities
Engage in simple and basic vocational and entrepreneurial activities that will help you earn and save money to support your parents.

Show proof of an increase in your 'Scout is Thrifty' savings account in your preferred bank or e-wallet provider.

Service Activities
Together with your Crew members, plan, organize, and conduct 'Scout's Good Turn' habits through school or community service projects related to environment, development, and peace.

Outdoor and Indoor Activities
Plan, organize, and conduct a 5–6 kilometers exploration with your Outfit. Explain to your fellow Scouts how to find directions with and without a compass, describe and predict weather and environmental conditions, and demonstrate safe exploration procedures.

Earn the following merit badges:
• Environment
• Weather
• First Aid
• Signaling
• Navigation

Outdoor Scout

Senior Scout Ideals
During your Crew and Outfit Meeting, explain in your own words the significance of the values you learned from the Scout Oath and Law, the Senior Scout Code, the Scout Motto, and the Senior Scout Slogan and how you relate it to your daily life.

In your Crew/Outfit Meeting, explain and discuss with your fellow Scouts how to show respect for Scouts and friends with other faiths or religious beliefs.

Participate in activities that will show your citizenship, civic-consciousness, and patriotism.

Social Activities
Identify and explain to your parents, teachers, and Scout Leaders the importance of the Rights of the Child and Safeguarding Policy of the BSP.

In a Crew and Outfit Meeting, under the guidance of your Outfit Advisor, identify and explain to your fellow Scouts the physical characteristics and changes of adolescents and young people in the teenage group, including sexual maturity and reproductive health.

Explain and demonstrate how to properly introduce two or more persons of different age groups and of the opposite sex; cordially send and reply to formal and informal invitations and attend one formal affair with a person of the opposite sex or with a member of your Crew and demonstrate proper social graces and etiquette.

Satisfy your parents and Scout Leaders that you are in good physical condition to undertake outdoor high adventure activities and are continuously living a healthy lifestyle.

Vocational Activities
Earn at least three (3) Elective Merit Badges of your personal choice and interest.

Read at least 2–3 journals, books, or magazines featuring livelihood, vocational, and entrepreneurial articles of your own choice and interests. Write an essay about what you have learned from the readings.

Show proof of increase in your 'Scout is Thrifty' savings account in your preferred bank or e-wallet provider.

Service Activities
Identify local environmental issues and propose potential solutions. Plan and execute an environmental project, understanding its connections from local to global levels. Evaluate the project's impact on the Scouts, the community, and the environment. Collaborate with your Crew members to plan, organize, lead, initiate, and/or actively participate in at least one environmental service project (e.g., Earth Day, Coastal Clean-Up, Earth Hour, recycling initiative, solid waste management programs, tree planting, etc.).

Outdoor and Indoor Activities
Identify different types of environmental hazards and natural disasters and explain their causes. Demonstrate how to help others prepare for and respond to environmental hazards (e.g., fires, earthquakes, landslides, flash floods, vehicular accidents) and natural disasters prevalent in the local area. Explain how environmental changes can influence the occurrence and severity of these hazards and disasters.

Actively participate in an emergency preparedness drill in your school and community or barangay on a regular basis.

Earn the following merit badges:
• Camping
• Swimming
• Emergency Preparedness

Venturer

Senior Scout Ideals
Satisfy your parents, teachers, Scout Leaders, and peers that you are living up to the Scout Oath and Law, the Scout Motto, and the Senior Scout Slogan.

Show to your fellow Scouts and Leaders that you have abided by the Senior Scout Code in all your dealings.

Take active participation in church activities with a friend or fellow Scout of the same faith.

Discuss ways and means to develop nationalism with younger Scouts and lower ranks.

Social Activities
Make a survey of values, beliefs, and practices in your community. Examine their importance to the life of the people in the community. Analyze these values and beliefs in relation to the Scout Oath and Law and determine which values or beliefs need to be changed, improved, or modified to make a better community. Present this to your Crew.

Together with your Crew and Outfit Members, plan, organize, and conduct your Annual Outfit Scout Youth Forum. Invite members of your school student government council (SSG) or Sangguniang Kabataan (SK) to discuss issues and challenges affecting the lives of young people in the community.

Vocational Activities
Consult with your Outfit Advisor and a Merit Badge Counselor to arrange an interview with a lawyer, doctor, engineer, police officer, or another licensed professional of your choice. Write an essay reflecting on your insights gained from the interview.

Earn five (5) Elective Merit Badges that align with your chosen Advancement Track (Land, Air, or Sea Scouting).

Land: Hiking, Nature Lore, Wildlife Conservation, Forestry, Pioneering
Air: Astronomy, Aviation, Electricity, Electronics, Radio
Sea: Seamanship, Boating, Snorkeling, Fishing, Radio

Show proof of increasing your 'Scout is Thrifty' savings account in your preferred bank or e-wallet provider.

Service Activities
Working with your Crew, plan, organize, and conduct a community service project. Document the project and its impact, ensuring it aligns with one of the key areas of service: Environment, Development, or Peace Education.

Outdoor and Indoor Activities
Together with your Crew and Outfit, actively join, train, and participate in your School and Community or Barangay Disaster Risk Reduction Management (DRRM) Group or Patrol in coordination with School Officials and Local Government Units.

Actively join and participate in training courses on Emergency Preparedness, Safety, and Life Saving with the BSP's Emergency Service Training Course (ESTC), LGU's DRRM Offices, the Philippine Red Cross, and other agencies, and organize your Outfit Emergency Service Corps.

Eagle Scout

Senior Scout Ideals
Create a post or series of posts on your social media account(s) that demonstrate how you are living by the Scout Promise, Scout Law, Scout Motto, and Senior Scout Slogan. Use your posts to showcase acts of kindness, responsibility, helpfulness, and community service that align with these values.

Prove to your fellow Scouts and Leaders that you are living up to the Senior Scout Code as your personal code of conduct in your daily lives.

Create a series of social media posts that show how you're fulfilling your 'Duty to God' through religious activities. Share experiences like attending services, volunteering, or helping in your community, with captions that reflect the values you're embracing.

Together with your fellow Scouts in the Crew and Outfit, plan, organize, and conduct a 'Worn-Out Flag Disposal Ceremony'.

Social Activities
Under the supervision of your Outfit Advisors, together with the members of your Crew and Outfit, plan, organize, and conduct a cultural or social activity, either indoors or outdoors, involving members of the opposite sex.

Vocational Activities
Attend and actively participate in a Career Orientation Forum, Seminar, or Symposium for Students conducted by your school and/or organized by any of your preferred colleges or universities and write your reflection about this activity.

Discuss with your Outfit Advisor, teacher, or guidance counselor about your strengths, hobbies, and interests. Explain to them your 3-Year Vocational or Career Path Plan and seek advice for career path planning.

Show proof that your 'Scout is Thrifty' savings account is in good standing and discuss with your parents and teachers how you intend to increase your savings for the future.

Service Activities
Together with your Crew members, plan, organize, and conduct an Eagle Scout Service Project. Document the project and its impact, ensuring it contributes meaningfully to the community.

Outdoor and Indoor Activities
Together with your Crew and Outfit, volunteer to plan, organize, and lead an emergency preparedness drill in your school and/or community and demonstrate how to transport an injured person and give first aid to simple and complex fractures and severe bleeding.

In consultation with your Outfit Advisor, plan, organize, and go for a 3-day and 2-night survival expedition camp in any of the BSP campsite or government-accredited natural parks and wildlife areas and show proof that you have managed to keep yourself dry, safe, healthy, and in good condition. Make an essay about this experience.

Participate in at least one (Council, Regional, or National) Jamboree and document your experience by creating a series of social media posts and reflect.

Earn the following merit badges:
• Life Saving
• Global Citizenship

In addition, earn three (3) Career Elective Merit Badges
